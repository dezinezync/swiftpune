Code of conduct.

SwiftPune is a community dedicated to fostering learning, collaboration, and mutual respect among the people who build on Apple's platforms — iOS, macOS, visionOS, and server-side Swift. We're engineers, designers, and students, and we want every meetup, campus session, and online conversation to feel welcoming and inclusive. By taking part in SwiftPune, you agree to follow these community guidelines and code of conduct.

01 Be respectful and inclusive

  • Treat everyone with kindness, dignity, and respect.
  • Discrimination, harassment, or exclusionary behaviour based on race, gender, sexual orientation, disability, age, religion, caste, or any other characteristic is strictly prohibited.
  • Encourage participation from everyone, regardless of experience level — the newest person in the room is the most important person in the room.

02 No harassment or hate speech

  • SwiftPune is a harassment-free space. Harassment includes, but is not limited to, offensive comments, intimidation, stalking, sustained disruption, or unwelcome advances.
  • Hate speech, personal attacks, and toxic behaviour will not be tolerated.
  • This includes jokes about sex, sexuality, race, religion, nationality, caste, or body size; showing content that is objectionable or unlawful under Indian law in public; or continuing to interact with someone after they've asked you to stop.
  • Sustained disruption of talks or events, inappropriate physical contact, and unwelcome sexual attention are never acceptable.

03 Keep it professional and friendly

  • Keep your language professional and free of explicit or abusive words across all community interactions — whether at meetups or in our online spaces (WhatsApp, LinkedIn, and social media discussions).
  • Maintain a positive and constructive tone, even when giving criticism or feedback.
  • Disagreements are natural, but should be handled with professionalism and courtesy.

04 Stay on-topic

  • Keep discussions relevant to Swift, Apple-platform development (iOS, macOS, visionOS, watchOS), and related technologies.
  • Take off-topic conversations to a more appropriate channel, or skip them if they don't align with the community's purpose.

05 Respect privacy and confidentiality

  • Do not share anyone's private information, personal details, or confidential content without their explicit consent.
  • Do not take screenshots or record conversations or talks without permission.

06 No spam or self-promotion abuse

  • A little self-promotion is fine — sharing your projects, blog posts, or work is welcome when it's relevant and not overdone.
  • No spamming, advertising, or unsolicited promotion of products or services unrelated to the community.

07 Follow event and platform rules

  • At SwiftPune meetups, campus sessions, and community nights, follow the guidance of the organisers and the venue or host.
  • Respect the terms of service of the platforms we use (WhatsApp, LinkedIn, Instagram, X, Mastodon, Luma, and others).

08 Reporting violations

  • If you witness or experience any behaviour that violates these guidelines, please report it to a member of the SwiftPune core team straight away, or email community@swiftpune.org. Reports are handled confidentially and taken seriously.
  • If someone is bothering you, tell the organisers immediately and we'll take care of it. We want SwiftPune to stay a room everyone's glad they walked into.

09 Consequences of violations

  • Depending on the severity of the offence, violations of this code of conduct may result in a warning, a temporary suspension, or a permanent ban from the community and its events.

By participating in SwiftPune, you agree to abide by these community guidelines and code of conduct. Let's build a positive, welcoming, and respectful community together. For any questions or concerns, reach out to the SwiftPune core team.

Report a concern

This policy applies at all SwiftPune meetups, campus sessions, workshops, watch parties, and community-related social events, as well as in our online spaces.
