// crew

Built by the people building on Apple's platforms.

SwiftPune brings together Swift developers in Pune — to learn from each other, share what we know, and grow together. Mostly working devs from Pune's iOS and macOS teams. A growing number of students. All here because shipping software is more fun in a room of people who get it.

We're independent, volunteer-run, and free. We're serious about the craft and gentle with each other. The room is small enough for hallway conversations to actually happen, and the bar is "did you learn something" — not "did you impress anyone."

// meetup()

Find us on a weekend morning.

Each event lands on LinkedIn and Luma a couple of weeks ahead. The fastest heads-up is the WhatsApp group — that's where times, venues, and RSVPs drop first. A typical session runs ~3 hours: three talks, a refreshments break, and a hands-on lab to close.

// first.commit

Coming for the first time?

Two minutes of reading. Four things to know before you walk in — in the order they'll happen.

  1. 01

    // pack

    Bring a laptop, your questions, an opinion.

    The opinion is the important one. Even a wrong one starts a better conversation than no opinion.

  2. 02

    // wear

    Whatever you'd wear to work or to a college fest.

    Both fit. We're not a uniform crowd.

  3. 03

    // hello round

    We open with introductions.

    Name, what you build (or want to build), one thing you're stuck on this week. Nobody's a stranger by talk #1.

  4. 04

    // on the house

    Refreshments are taken care of.

    A spread to keep the morning going. Mention any dietary preferences or allergies on the RSVP and we'll sort it.

// sponsors

Supported by people who care.

Venues, food, name tags, the small AV bits — every meetup runs on someone's generosity. Hosts and sponsors get a stage minute at the event they back and a real thank-you from the room. Two companies have made it possible so far; the next slot could be yours.

notes/to_future_hosts.swift

A meetup needs almost nothing — a room for 25,

a projector, a few pots of coffee, three hours

of a weekend morning. We can't really sell

anyone on this. There's nothing to buy.

Past hosts have offered what they already had:

the conference room that was empty that morning,

the coffee budget no one was watching. The

talks were ours; the chairs were theirs.

That was the whole deal.

If you'd like to make a morning happen, write

to us. We'll shape it around whatever you can

offer. No tier list. No deck. No ask beyond

the room.

— the SwiftPune crew
